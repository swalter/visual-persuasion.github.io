Call for Papers

This workshop focuses on how expressing different types of non-at-issue content visually (e.g. through gestures) interacts with linguistic information in shaping commitment and persuasion, particularly in persuasive text types such as advertisements, political communication, and instructional media. These non-at-issue elements often operate beneath the surface of explicit messages but are argued to play a crucial role in influencing attitudes, beliefs, and decision-making (Saarinen 2008, Li & Sun 2018, Vallauri 2021, Vallauri et al. 2021, Moldovan 2023, Thoma et al. 2023). However, open issues remain regarding the theoretical basis for these effects, and explicit models of how non-at-issue content arises in visually presented persuasive speech are missing. Our workshop aims to fill this gap. The event will bring together researchers and practitioners from theoretical and applied fields to explore experimental methods for studying how visual and linguistic elements interact in persuasive contexts. Key questions include:

  • How do gestures, images, and other visual cues amplify or undermine verbal messages?
  • How does visual input influence the level of commitment of speakers to the verbal message?
  • What role does non-at-issue content play in persuasive strategies across text types?
  • How can experimental approaches advance applied linguistics by addressing real-world issues like misinformation, audience engagement, and cultural variation in communication?

This event aims to provide new perspectives and tools for analyzing and applying persuasive strategies in multimodal communication. The workshop is particularly relevant for applied linguistics, as it bridges theoretical insights with practical concerns in education, marketing, and public discourse.
